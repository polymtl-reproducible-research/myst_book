# Introduction

:::{important}
**Submission deadline:** *To be determined*  
**Team formation:** This laboratory must be completed in teams of two students.
:::

Throughout this course, you have progressively developed the skills required to conduct and share reproducible research.

The purpose of this final laboratory is to reflect on these experiences and discuss how they contribute to reproducible and sustainable scientific research.

---

# Presentation

- The presentation must be prepared using **Google Slides**.
- The presentation duration must be **between 8 and 10 minutes**.
- Both team members must actively participate in the presentation.
- A **question period** will follow each presentation. Questions may be asked by classmates and the teaching staff.

## Structure

:::{important}
This presentation is intended to be a reflection on your experience throughout the course rather than a demonstration of technical tools.

Strong presentations will move beyond describing what was done and will instead explain:

* Why specific practices were adopted;
* What challenges were encountered;
* What lessons were learned;
* How these experiences relate to reproducible and sustainable scientific research.
:::

### Introduction 
Introduce your team and your research project. 

Topics to address:

- What was the goal of your research project?
- What artifacts were produced (data, code, documentation, etc.)?
- How did your understanding of reproducible research evolve throughout the course?

### Project Experience
Critically analyze the project developed by your team.

:::{hint}
**Focus**: your repository, your workflow, your decisions.
:::

Topics to address:

- Good design choices
- Challenges encountered during development
- Problems discovered during evaluation
- Repository organization and management
- Collaboration workflow and branching strategy
- What would you do differently if repeating the project?

### Open Science in pratice
Reflect on how the activities performed throughout the course relate to real-world scientific collaboration and reproducible research.

:::{hint}
Focus: what your experience reveals about how research should be organized, shared, evaluated, and maintained.
:::

Topics to address:

- Dissemination of Research Artifacts
- Scientific Collaboration
- External Review and Feedback
- Sustainability of a Reproducible Project
- Benefits and Limitations

### Cost of reproductibility
Reflect on the effort required to develop a reproducible project.

Topics to address:

- Additional effort required
- Documentation burden
- Repository maintenance
- Technical and documentation debt
- Trade-offs between speed and reproducibility

### Conclusion

Summarize your main takeaways from the course.

Topics to address:

- Recommandations for futur students
- Which activity had the greatest impact on your understanding?
